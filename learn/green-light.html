The Calmlyte spectrum

Calmlyte is built around a narrow band of green light. Here is a plain introduction to what that means — where green sits in the visible spectrum, what "520–530 nm" refers to, and how the color of light shapes the way a room feels.

Light is a spectrum

The light we can see is a small band of the electromagnetic spectrum. Within it, wavelength determines color: shorter wavelengths appear violet and blue, the middle of the range appears green, and longer wavelengths appear amber and red. Wavelength is measured in nanometers (nm).

~400 nm violet~550 nm green~700 nm red

Calmlyte's core sits in the green band, around 520–530 nm.

Where green sits

Green occupies the middle of the visible range. Human daytime (photopic) vision is most sensitive to light near the green part of the spectrum, at roughly 555 nm — which is a well-established fact of human color vision and the reason green often reads as bright and easy to look at. Calmlyte's narrow-band green sits just below that peak, in the 520–530 nm range.

What "narrow-band 520–530 nm" means

Many white light sources emit across the whole visible spectrum at once. A narrow-band source concentrates its output in a small slice of wavelengths. Calmlyte's green is narrow-band, meaning it produces a specific, consistent green rather than a broad white. Calmlyte pairs this green core with two supporting tones used in different settings: a cooler 490–500 nm cyan-green and a warmer 590 nm amber.

Color and the feel of a room

The color and intensity of light change how a space reads. Bright, cool, blue-heavy light tends to feel alert and office-like; warm, low, amber light tends to feel soft and settled, the quality of light you notice near sunset. This is why the same room can feel completely different under different lighting.

Calmlyte is designed around this idea: a green-forward light meant to change the feel of a room, with settings that move from a balanced daytime character to a warmer, lower evening one.

The four Calmlyte settings

Each setting is a different balance of the same spectrum. Haven is balanced and everyday. Focus is brighter and slightly cooler. Stillness is soft and diffused. Dusk is warm and low. Together they let one light shift its character through the day.
